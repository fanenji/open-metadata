The schema fights you on day three. The first version of your CLAUDE.md will be too abstract. After a few ingests, you will notice the model is creating pages with slightly inconsistent structure — some have a “Key Claims” section, some have “Main Arguments,” and they mean the same thing. Fix the schema. Standardise the section names. The model will follow whatever the schema says, which means the schema needs to say the right thing clearly. The schema feels overengineered at first. With 10 pages, defining 5 page types and 8 lint rules seems like overkill. Past 50 pages, you will be grateful for the consistency.
