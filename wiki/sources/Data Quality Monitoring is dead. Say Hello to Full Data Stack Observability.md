# Data Quality Monitoring is dead. Say Hello to Full Data Stack Observability

This article argues that traditional data quality monitoring and testing are insufficient for modern, complex data stacks. It introduces **data observability** as the necessary evolution, defining it as the ability to gain actionable insights about data health across the entire pipeline. The authors propose a **Full Data Stack Observability** framework built on three pillars: **Metrics**, **Metadata**, and **Lineage** — adapted from software observability's Metrics, Traces, and Logs.

Key applications include anomaly detection (freshness, completeness, duplication, schema, accuracy), lineage-driven root cause analysis, incident management with impact assessment, and post-mortem analysis to shift from firefighting to fire prevention. The article positions Sifflet as the first Full Data Stack Observability platform.

**Note:** The authors are the CEO and a team member of Sifflet, so the article should be read as a vendor perspective on the category.
