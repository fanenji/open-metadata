## Main Arguments & Findings

**Core claim:** The data catalog failure narrative is built on weak, commercially motivated evidence, not rigorous empirical research.

**Supporting evidence:**
1. The "30% adoption" statistic traces to vendor content (Promethium), not peer-reviewed research
2. The "350% ROI" claim comes from a vendor ROI calculator (ThinkData Works)
3. Academic literature (2006–2023 systematic review) focuses on requirements/preferences, not outcomes
4. Practitioner evidence is anecdotal (blog posts, consulting summaries) — high narrative value, low rigor

**Evidence strength:** **Moderate.** The document makes a valid methodological critique but doesn't itself provide counter-evidence. It identifies the gap rather than filling it. The systematic literature review [13] is the strongest source; vendor sources [1][1
