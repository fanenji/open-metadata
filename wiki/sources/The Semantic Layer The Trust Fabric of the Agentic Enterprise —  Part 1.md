# The Semantic Layer: The Trust Fabric of the Agentic Enterprise — Part 1

This article by [[Sriram Narasimhan]] (VP Engineering, Data & Analytics at SAP Procurement) argues that a governed, machine-readable semantic layer is the essential foundation for trustworthy, explainable agentic AI systems in the enterprise.

## Key Arguments

- Enterprise AI is moving to an **agentic paradigm** where systems act, not just answer.
- The #1 complaint against enterprise AI is lack of trust and explainability.
- A **semantic layer** — a governed model of business entities, metrics, and relationships — provides consistency, interoperability, and traceability.
- The next wave of AI, **SynthAI** (Synthesis AI), requires semantic understanding to synthesize vast data into actionable insights, not just generate content.

## The Evolution of Business Semantics

The article frames four eras:
1. **Dashboards → Visibility**: Early BI with inconsistent metric definitions.
2. **Metrics Layers → Standardization**: Centralized metric definitions but no entity understanding.
3. **Semantic Layers → Understanding**: Shared model connecting entities and relationships.
4. **Agentic Layers → Autonomy**: Copilots and agents acting with full lineage and explainability.

## Practical Recommendations

- Define top entities and metrics.
- Expose them via data products or APIs.
- Govern ownership and lineage.
- Pilot a copilot that consumes those semantics.

## Series Structure

- Part 1 (this article): **Why** business apps need a Semantic Layer.
- Part 2: **What** are the various forms of capturing business semantics (ontologies, knowledge graphs, etc.).
- Part 3: **How** — comparison of tools (Looker, dbt Metrics, Palantir Ontology, Cube, SAP, AI startups).
