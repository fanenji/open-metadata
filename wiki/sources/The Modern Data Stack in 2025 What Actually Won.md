# The Modern Data Stack in 2025: What Actually Won

A data-driven analysis of which tools dominate the modern data ecosystem — and why. Based on industry surveys (15,000+ respondents), 50,000+ job postings, vendor signals, and practitioner surveys.

## Key Findings

- **dbt achieved near-monopoly** in transformation (73% adoption, 90%+ in teams >5 practitioners).
- **Databricks is the fastest-growing warehouse** (+60% YoY), driven by Unity Catalog, Delta Lake maturity, and lakehouse messaging.
- **Multi-warehouse is the new normal** — 38% of companies use 2+ warehouses.
- **Airflow is declining** (-12.5% YoY) due to complexity and alternatives maturing.
- **Reverse ETL adoption slower than predicted** (22% vs 35% predicted), likely to consolidate into ingestion tools.
- **Data observability SaaS tools struggling** — DIY + dbt tests dominate (42% adoption).
- **SQL remains king** — 94% of transformation logic is SQL, not Python.
- **BI market unchanged** — Tableau and Power BI held ground; "new breed" tools (Mode, Sigma, Hex) failed to disrupt.

## Failed Predictions

- "Fivetran will be replaced by open-source" — Still 34% market share.
- "BI will consolidate to 2–3 winners" — Still fragmented.
- "DataOps will be a major category" — Never materialized.
- "Everything moves to real-time" — Batch still dominant (80%+).
- "Data mesh will transform organizations" — Minimal adoption (<5%).

## Predictions for 2026–2027

- dbt will hit 85%+ adoption.
- Multi-warehouse standard: 50%+ of companies will use 2+ warehouses.
- Databricks will overtake Snowflake in growth rate.
- Airflow below 35% adoption.
- Reverse ETL consolidates into ingestion tools.
- AI-generated SQL becomes common (40%+ of queries AI-assisted).
- Data observability remains DIY.
- Potential major acquisition (Databricks or Snowflake buys dbt Labs).
- Potential warehouse cost crisis forcing architectural changes.
