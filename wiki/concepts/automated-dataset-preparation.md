# Automated Dataset Preparation

Automated dataset preparation refers to the process of generating training datasets for geospatial AI models, including the creation of image chips and corresponding labels from remote sensing data. This capability is critical for lowering the barrier to entry for geospatial AI applications.

Key aspects include:
- Streamlined access to satellite and aerial imagery from providers like Sentinel, Landsat, and NAIP
- Tools for downloading, mosaicking, and preprocessing remote sensing data
- Automated generation of training datasets with image chips and labels
- Vector-to-raster and raster-to-vector conversion utilities optimized for AI workflows
- Data augmentation techniques specific to geospatial data
- Support for integrating [[Overture Maps]] data and other open datasets
