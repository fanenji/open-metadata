# Graph-of-Thought (GoT)

**Graph-of-Thought (GoT)** is an advanced reasoning paradigm that models thoughts as vertices in a graph, allowing for more complex cognitive processes than linear or tree-based models.

## Comparison to Previous Paradigms

| Paradigm | Structure | Capabilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** | Linear | Sequential reasoning steps. |
| **Tree-of-Thought (ToT)** | Tree | Backtracking and lookahead. |
| **Graph-of-Thought (GoT)** | Graph | **Cyclic reasoning**, thought aggregation, and feedback loops. |

## Advantages
- **Performance**: Achieves significant improvements (e.g., 62% over ToT) on complex tasks.
- **Efficiency**: Reduces computational costs by optimizing the search space through graph-based dependencies.
- **Complexity**: Enables the modeling of real-world problem-solving, which is rarely linear.
