# SynthAI (Synthesis AI)

**SynthAI** (Synthesis AI) is a term coined by a16z describing the next wave of enterprise AI that synthesizes vast amounts of data into concise, actionable insights — as opposed to GenAI which focuses on generating more content.

## GenAI vs. SynthAI

| Aspect | GenAI | SynthAI |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Goal | Generate content (emails, reports, drafts) | Synthesize data into insights and recommendations |
| Direction | Divergent (expanding information) | Convergent (focusing information) |
| Example | Summarize a contract | Explain which suppliers are due for renewal, flag risks, recommend actions |
| Requirement | Language understanding | Semantic understanding of business concepts |

## Why Semantics Matter

Synthesis requires understanding. To reason over "Supplier Risk" or "Contract Value," AI must know these aren't just database columns — they're governed business concepts with specific definitions, calculations, and relationships. Without a [[semantic-layer]], even advanced AI remains a sophisticated summarizer, not a trusted business advisor.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

- The [[semantic-layer]] provides the semantic foundation SynthAI requires.
- The [[enterprise-ai-trust-gap]] is especially acute for SynthAI systems that make recommendations.
- The [[agentic-enterprise]] paradigm is where SynthAI operates.
