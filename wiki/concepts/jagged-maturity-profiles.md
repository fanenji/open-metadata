# Jagged Maturity Profiles

A **jagged maturity profile** describes an organization that exhibits non-linear maturity across the different dimensions of a [[data-mesh-maturity-assessment]]. For example, an organization may have a highly mature [[self-serve-data-platform]] (Phase 3) but struggle with basic [[data-domain-governance|domain ownership]] (Phase 1).

## Why It Matters

- Most maturity frameworks assume linear progression across all dimensions
- In practice, organizations rarely advance uniformly
- A jagged profile requires a tailored improvement roadmap, not a one-size-fits-all approach
- Scoring an organization with a single "maturity score" obscures critical gaps

## Common Patterns

- **Platform-Rich, Domain-Poor:** Heavy investment in technology without establishing domain ownership leads to underutilized platform
- **Governance-Heavy, Product-Light:** Strong compliance and security controls but few published [[data-product-definition|data products]]
- **Culture-Forward, Infrastructure-Behind:** Enthusiastic domain teams but inadequate [[self-serve-data-platform]] to support them

## Implications for Assessment

- Assessments should report maturity per dimension, not as a single score
- Improvement plans should prioritize the weakest dimension
- Organizations should not expect to progress uniformly through [[data-mesh-maturity-phases]]
