# The Agentic Enterprise Architecture

The **Agentic Enterprise Architecture** describes a paradigm shift in organizational data usage, moving from human-centric BI to autonomous, agent-centric decision-making.

## Core Components
The architecture relies on a "trust fabric" composed of four interdependent layers:
*   **Data Substrate**: The execution foundation (e.g., [[data-lakehouse]]).
*   **Ontologies**: The layer of shared, explicit meaning.
*   **Knowledge Graphs**: The layer of contextual relationships and causality.
*   **Metrics Layers**: The layer of executable business truth and semantic contracts.

## The Decision Pipeline
Unlike traditional architectures that focus on a **Data Pipeline** (the movement and transformation of data), the Agentic Enterprise focuses on a **Decision Pipeline**. The objective is to move from raw data to actionable, governed business outcomes where AI agents can act with high confidence and low risk.

## Key Shift: Descriptive to Prescriptive
In traditional architectures, semantics is **des/criptive**—it explains what happened in the past. In an agentic architecture, semantics becomes **prescriptive**—it defines the boundaries, rules, and permissions for what an autonomous agent is allowed to do.
