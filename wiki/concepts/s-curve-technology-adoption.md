# S-Curve of Technology Adoption

A framework for understanding how technologies go through cycles of development and deployment, most effectively described by economist Carlotta Perez in her foundational 2010 paper. The S-curve model explains the pattern of rapid innovation followed by maturation that characterizes technological change.

## The Framework

Each technology goes through its own "S" curve:

1. **Development Phase**: Rapid innovation as the technology is first created and refined. Early adopters are forgiving, and the focus is on proving the concept and unlocking new capabilities.

2. **Deployment Phase**: The technology matures as it is adopted by larger audiences. Improvements focus on reliability, coverage, and performance rather than fundamental UX changes. This phase is necessary for the technology to become a stable foundation for further innovation.

3. **Maturity**: The technology reaches its full potential and becomes a commodity.

## Application to the Modern Data Stack

Tristan Handy applied this framework to the modern data stack in his 2020 article:

- **Development (2005-2012)**: Early MPP databases like Vertica, culminating in Redshift's launch
- **Deployment (2016-2020)**: Core products (Fivetran, dbt, Looker, Snowflake) improved reliability and coverage without changing core UX
- **Next Wave (2021-2025)**: The now-mature foundation enables a new round of innovation in governance, real-time, reverse ETL, democratized exploration, and vertical analytics

## Historical Examples

The article cites the telegraph as an example: Thomas Edison invented a quadruplex telegraph in 1874 that enabled Western Union to quadruple throughput on existing lines — "same telegraph, more throughput" — illustrating the deployment phase dynamic.
