# Agentic Enterprise

The **agentic enterprise** is an organizational paradigm where AI systems act autonomously — making recommendations, taking decisions, and executing workflows — rather than simply answering questions or generating content. This represents a shift from passive AI tools to active AI agents.

## Key Characteristics

- **Autonomous action**: Systems that can act on behalf of users and lines of business.
- **Context-aware reasoning**: Agents navigate real business relationships (e.g., Customer→Order→Invoice) rather than guessing from raw fields.
- **Explainable outcomes**: Every recommendation can be traced back through metric definitions and data lineage.
- **Governed logic**: Actions are grounded in rules and metrics that the organization controls and audits.

## The Trust Challenge

The #1 complaint against enterprise AI is lack of trust and explainability. Without a [[semantic layer]], even advanced AI remains a black box — eroding confidence and slowing adoption.
