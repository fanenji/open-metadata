# Business Semantics Evolution

The **Business Semantics Evolution** framework describes four distinct eras in how organizations capture and use business meaning in data. Each era solves a different pain point and builds toward the next.

## The Four Eras

### 1. Dashboards → Visibility
Early BI tools gave every team its own version of truth. Finance, Sales, and Procurement each defined metrics differently. Visibility improved, but consistency suffered.

### 2. Metrics Layers → Standardization
Centralized metric definitions brought order. Teams agreed on how to calculate margin or savings %, yet systems still lacked an understanding of what entities like Supplier or Contract actually meant.

### 3. Semantic Layers → Understanding
A shared model connects business entities and relationships — Supplier → PO → Invoice → Category Spend. Both humans and AI can reason using the same vocabulary. This is the era of the [[semantic-layer]].

### 4. Agentic Layers → Autonomy
The next frontier transforms understanding into action. Copilots and agents can propose sourcing changes, flag anomalies, or forecast outcomes — and explain their reasoning with full lineage and context. This is the [[agentic-enterprise]] era.

## Key Insight

Organizations that master semantic grounding today will lead in tomorrow's era of explainable, agentic business systems.
