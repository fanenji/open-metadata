# Analytics Engineering Role

Analytics engineering is the practice of creating data models that accurately represent the business and are optimized for a variety of downstream consumers. The analytics engineer role sits between the data engineer and the data analyst, bridging the gap between technical data infrastructure and business analytics.

[[dbt Labs]] popularized this role through the [[dbt Core]] framework, which provides a complete playground for analytics teams. The role is relatively new, and practitioners often come from data analytics backgrounds without extensive software engineering experience. dbt is designed to bring software engineering best practices (modularity, testing, version control, documentation) to these teams.

Key responsibilities include:
- Building and maintaining data models that represent business entities and processes
- Defining sources, transformations, and tests within a dbt project
- Optimizing data models for downstream consumption by analysts, BI tools, and applications
- Applying software engineering practices to data transformation workflows

This concept is closely related to the [[context-architect-role]] documented elsewhere in the wiki, as both roles focus on creating meaning and structure from raw data.
