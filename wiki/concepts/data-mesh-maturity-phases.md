# Data Mesh Maturity Phases

Synthesizing the available literature, the typical progression of [[data-mesh]] maturity can be defined across five phases.

## Phase 0: Monolithic / Centralized

- **Duration:** N/A (Baseline)
- **Key Characteristics:** Single data team is a bottleneck. Manual governance. High friction for data access. Centralized [[monolithic-data-lake]].

## Phase 1: Foundation / Pilot

- **Duration:** 3–6 months
- **Key Characteristics:** 2–3 pilot domains selected. First [[data-product-definition|data products]] defined. Basic platform provisioned.

## Phase 2: Formalization / Platform

- **Duration:** 6–12 months
- **Key Characteristics:** Mature [[self-serve-data-platform]]. [[data-contract-platform|Data contracts]] and SLAs defined. Enablement team formed. Governance rules exist but may be manual.

## Phase 3: Scale / Expansion

- **Duration:** 12–18 months
- **Key Characteristics:** Systematic onboarding of new domains. Platform handles growing complexity. Governance becomes increasingly automated (computational).

## Phase 4: Optimization / Ecosystem

- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Key Characteristics:** Mesh is the standard operating model. Governance is fully automated. Focus shifts to optimizing the platform and fostering an ecosystem of reusable data products.

## Key Insights

- Progression is not always linear; organizations may exhibit [[data-mesh-challenges|jagged maturity profiles]]
- Each phase builds on the previous; skipping phases leads to failure
- Duration estimates are approximate and depend on organizational context
- The assessment should inform which phase an organization is in and what the next step should be
