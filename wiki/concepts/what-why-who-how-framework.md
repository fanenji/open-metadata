# What, Why, Who, and How Framework

A multidimensional approach to metadata designed to make data "AI-ready." Unlike traditional semantic layers that only focus on the technical "How," this framework requires capturing the full business context.

### Dimensions
- **How**: The technical implementation, such as SQL logic, joins, and mathematical formulas.
- **What**: The business definition (e.g., "Churn is defined as...").
- **Why**: The business purpose, intent, and the downstream consequences of the data (e.g., "To monitor product health and trigger interventions").
- **Who**: The ownership, stakeholders, and primary contacts for the data asset.

This framework is essential for AI agents to understand the *intent* behind data, allowing them to make decisions that align with business goals rather than just executing queries.
