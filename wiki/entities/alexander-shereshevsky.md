# Alexander Shereshevsky

Alexander Shereshevsky is a knowledge graph engineer and the primary architect and proponent of the [[Agentic Graph Framework]], whose work focuses on the intersection of knowledge graph theory and production reality for enterprise architectures in GraphRAG and autonomous agents. He has practical experience building knowledge graphs across financial services, pharma, and legal tech. He writes for [[Graph Praxis]] on Medium, a publication devoted to production knowledge graph architectures, dynamic ontologies, and AI agents.

## Key Contributions

- Primary architect and proponent of the [[Agentic Graph Framework]].
- Author of the "Knowledge Graphs for Enterprise AI Memory" series.
- Proposes the **90/10 heuristic**: property graphs for 90% of AI memory use cases, RDF/OWL for the remaining 10%.
- Advocates for [[application-layer-schema-enforcement]] as a practical middle ground between flexibility and rigor.
- Recommends [[FalkorDB]] for enterprise AI memory systems.
