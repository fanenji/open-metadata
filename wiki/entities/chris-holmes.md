# Chris Holmes

Chris Holmes is the creator of [[GeoParquet]] and a co-author of its specification. He is a Planet Fellow at [[Planet Labs]] and a Radiant Earth Technical Fellow. He is a leading evangelist for cloud-native geospatial formats and the primary author of the GeoParquet Ecosystem blog post. He advocates for storage-compute separation and open formats to enable multi-engine geospatial data access. He was a speaker on the livestream "Geospatial Tables in the Open Lakehouse: A New Era for Iceberg & Parquet," where he shared the origin story of GeoParquet and its role as a precursor to Iceberg GEO types.

## Contributions

- Co-started the GeoParquet standardization process at OGC
- Evangelized GeoParquet adoption across libraries, tools, and data providers
- Converted multiple datasets to GeoParquet (Google Buildings, Overture Maps, EuroCrops, NYC Taxi Zones)
- Active in the Cloud Native Geospatial community

## Relevance to Project

Chris Holmes' work provides authoritative guidance on the GeoParquet ecosystem, directly informing the project's evaluation of GeoParquet as a standard format.
