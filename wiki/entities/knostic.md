# Knostic

Knostic is a security research organization that conducted a scan of nearly 2,000 publicly accessible [[model-context-protocol]] (MCP) servers. Their research found that every verified instance granted access to internal tool listings without requiring any authentication, highlighting the widespread [[mcp-attack-surface]] problem.

## Key Findings
- 100% of ~2,000 verified MCP servers lacked authentication
- Published the article "MCP Security Issues and Best Practices You Need to Know"
- Their research is a primary source for the [[mcp-attack-surface]] concept
